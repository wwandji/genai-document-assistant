Pharmaceutical Supply Chain Overview

This document describes a simplified pharmaceutical supply chain process for a medicine distribution company.

The company sources pharmaceutical products from certified manufacturers and distributes them to pharmacies, hospitals, and healthcare providers across multiple European countries.

Key steps in the supply chain include:
- Supplier qualification and contract management
- Procurement of pharmaceutical products
- Storage under regulated temperature conditions
- Batch tracking and serialization
- Distribution to customers

Regulatory compliance is a critical aspect of the supply chain. All products must comply with GDP (Good Distribution Practice) guidelines. Documentation must be maintained for audits, including temperature logs, batch numbers, and delivery records.

Common challenges in the pharmaceutical supply chain include:
- Ensuring product availability
- Managing recalls efficiently
- Maintaining full traceability
- Reducing delivery delays

The company aims to improve transparency and efficiency by using digital tools and intelligent systems to analyze documentation and operational data.
